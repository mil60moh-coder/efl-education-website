Year 6 – English as a Foreign Language

Year 6 represents a key transition stage in primary education. Learners consolidate core language structures while developing greater confidence, accuracy, and independence in communication.

Pedagogical focus

  • Meaningful use of language in familiar contexts
  • Balanced development of listening, speaking, reading, and writing
  • Gradual reinforcement of grammar through use, not memorisation
  • Encouragement of learner autonomy and reflection

Programme structure

The Year 6 programme is organised into thematic units. Each unit integrates vocabulary, grammar, skills, and values in a coherent and progressive manner.

    Unit 1 – Entertaining myself
    Lesson 1: Let’s have fun

  • Unit 2 – People, places, and descriptions
  • Unit 3 – Food, health, and habits
  • Unit 4 – Leisure, sports, and interests
  • Unit 5 – Nature, environment, and responsibility
  • Unit 6 – Past experiences and simple narratives
  • Unit 7 – Future plans and personal aspirations

Assessment approach

Assessment in Year 6 is viewed as a continuous process. Activities, practice tasks, and tests are designed to support learning rather than interrupt it.

Self-check opportunities are provided to help learners monitor their own progress and develop responsibility for learning.

How to use this section

Teachers and learners may follow the units sequentially or focus on specific areas according to needs. Practice, tests, and self-check materials are aligned with the objectives of each unit.

Year 6 EFL content developed within a non-commercial, educational framework, grounded in curriculum expertise and classroom experience.
